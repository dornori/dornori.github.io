About Dornori

Where it started

Dornori was born at a beach gathering. A group of friends — guitars out, food spread across a blanket, the sky going dark — had no good light. Phones came out. White LED glare killed the mood. Someone said: "I just want to see your face."

That one moment became the Star‑A: a portable outdoor lamp that rises on command, runs on batteries, and looks like nothing you've seen before. A pole extends from a compact base — carrying a glowing UFO or Mushroom lamp head up to 190 cm into the air — driven by a real DC motor, controlled by a simple lever switch. No fire. No power outlet. No disposable gimmick. Something you can build yourself, or give to someone who will.

What makes it different

  • ✅ It moves. The pole rises. The lamp head climbs. There is nothing passive about it.
  • ✅ Real components. DC motor, Faston connectors, Wago lever terminals, gear assembly — the same classes of components used in automotive and domestic electronics.
  • ✅ Off-the-shelf. Every electronic component in the Star‑A is industry-standard and individually available. Nothing is proprietary. Nothing requires a trip back to us to replace.
  • ✅ Outdoors by design. 3× protected 18650 batteries. Compact base. Genuinely portable.
  • ✅ Two models. The UFO and the Mushroom — same electronics, completely different character.
  • ✅ Open files. STL, OBJ, 3MF, schematics, wiring diagrams — available for builders who want to go further.
  • ✅ Repairable. Every part available separately. No planned obsolescence.

Who builds (and uses) a Star‑A

The Star‑A was designed for builders aged 8–12 working with an adult. In practice, it finds its way to a lot of different people:

  • 🧒 Kids and teens — who want a light that goes with them and looks genuinely cool
  • 👨‍👧 Families — who want a weekend project that actually produces something worth using
  • 👴 Grandparents — who want to build something memorable with grandchildren
  • 🏫 Classrooms & STEM programmes — consistent, documentable, genuinely engaging
  • 🖨️ Makers with 3D printers — print, source, wire, build, take outside
  • 🌙 Anyone who wants portable outdoor light they actually built themselves

Three ways to get a Star‑A

  • Ready to use — fully assembled and tested. Load batteries, flip the lever.
  • Complete Assembly Kit — pre-printed parts, full electronics, build in 90 minutes. No printer, no soldering.
  • 3D Print Files — download and print every part yourself. Full DIY, full customisation.
