The build — 90 minutes of real engagement

The kit breaks into four natural stages that work well as a shared project:

  • 0–15 min — Discovery. Unpack everything. Lay it out. Identify the parts. The motor looks real because it is real. The Wago connectors look like they belong in something serious — they do.
  • 15–45 min — Mechanical assembly. Build the base. Mount the motor. Thread the tube through the gear housing. This is spatial reasoning, fine motor control, and mechanical thinking — happening naturally, not on a worksheet.
  • 45–70 min — Wiring. Connect the motor, lever switch, power switch, and LED lamp head using the colour-coded harness and Wago connectors. No soldering. No tools. But a real, complete DC circuit made with real components. "So when I flip this lever up... the motor gets power and spins which way?" Exactly.
  • 70–90 min — First light. Flip the lever. The motor hums. The tube rises. The glowing UFO or Mushroom climbs into the air above the table. "I made this."

For parents & educators — what they actually learn

The Star‑A kit is built around real components used in real electronics. Every learning outcome is a consequence of building something that genuinely works. See the full Parents & Educators guide for curriculum links and detailed STEM breakdown. Summary:

  • ✅ Complete DC circuits — how current flows from battery through switch to motor and LED. Traced by hand, with real wire.
  • ✅ Electromechanical systems — a motor converts electrical energy to rotational motion; gears convert that to vertical linear motion. Cause and effect, physical and electrical, simultaneously.
  • ✅ 3-position switching logic — Up: motor spins one direction (pole rises). Centre: open circuit (stops). Down: motor reverses (pole retracts). Real-world logic gate behaviour.
  • ✅ Wiring fundamentals — Faston crimp connectors, Wago push-in lever terminals, colour-coded harnesses. The same connection methods used in automotive and domestic electronics.
  • ✅ Mechanical assembly — gear housing, motor mounting, screw fastening. Spatial reasoning made physical.
  • ✅ Debugging mindset — "why isn't the tube moving?" is a real engineering question with a real answer they can find themselves.
  • ✅ Pride of authorship — this lamp goes to the park, the beach, or the next family gathering. Made by them. Used by them.
