The build — 90 minutes of real engagement

The kit breaks into four natural stages that work well as a shared project:

0–15 min
Discovery

Unpack everything. Lay it out. Identify the parts. The motor looks real because it is real. The Wago connectors look like they belong in something serious — they do.

15–45 min
Mechanical assembly

Build the base. Mount the motor. Thread the tube through the gear housing. This is spatial reasoning, fine motor control, and mechanical thinking — happening naturally, not on a worksheet.

45–70 min
Wiring

Connect the motor, lever switch, power switch, and LED lamp head using the colour-coded harness and Wago connectors. No soldering. No tools. But a real, complete DC circuit made with real components. "So when I flip this lever up… the motor gets power and spins which way?" Exactly.

70–90 min
First light

Flip the lever. The motor hums. The tube rises. The glowing UFO or Mushroom climbs into the air above the table. "I made this."
