Dornori started with a simple problem: a group of friends on the beach, no light that didn't break the mood. Star‑A is the answer.

Where it started

Dornori was born at a forest gathering. A group of friends sitting in a clearing — music playing, food spread across a blanket, the sky going dark — no trees around to hang a torch from. Phones came out. White LED glare killed the mood. Someone said: "I just want to see your face."

That one moment became the Star‑A: a portable outdoor lamp that rises on command, runs on batteries, and looks like nothing you've seen before. A pole extends from a compact base — carrying a glowing UFO or Mushroom lamp head up to 190 cm — driven by a DC motor, controlled by a simple lever switch.

No fire. No power outlet. No disposable gimmick. Ready to take with you, or something you can build yourself, there's 3 different option for different people.

A unique one of a kind product that is a must have for people that love the outdoors or that like to make things their own.

  • 🚀 It moves — rising pole, no other lamp like it
  • 🔋 3× protected 18650 batteries — truly portable
  • 🛠️ Industry components (Wago, Faston, DC motor)
  • 🖨️ Open source STL / OBJ / 3MF files free
  • 🍄 UFO + Mushroom · 4 colours each
  • ♻️ Repairable, every part available separately

Who builds (and uses) a Star‑A

Designed for builders aged 8–12 working with an adult. In practice, it finds its way to a lot of different people:

  • 🧒 Kids and teens — who want a light that goes with them and looks genuinely cool
  • 👨‍👧 Families — who want a weekend project that produces something worth using
  • 👴 Grandparents — who want to build something memorable with grandchildren
  • 🏫 Classrooms & STEM — consistent, documentable, genuinely engaging
  • 🖨️ Makers with 3D printers — print, source, wire, build, take outside

"There's something that happens the first time a child flips the lever and watches the pole rise. Their eyes go wide. Then they say: Wait — I made that? That's the moment. That's what Dornori is for."
