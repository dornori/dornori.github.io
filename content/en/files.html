For parents, grandparents & makers building with kids

The 3D Print Files route is the full maker journey. Printing the parts yourself — watching them come off the bed, cleaning them up, fitting them together — adds a layer of ownership that no pre-printed kit can replicate. Children who print their own parts understand the geometry of what they're building before they start assembly. It's also the most customisable path: colour, material, finish — all your choices.

The recommended age for the full print-and-build project is 10+ with an adult. Younger children can absolutely participate in the build stages — the Wago connectors require only finger pressure, and the assembly guide is fully visual. The printing itself is best supervised by an adult.

For educators & makerspaces

The print files work well as a multi-week classroom project: print over two weeks of lunch breaks, assemble and wire in a 90-minute session. Each student (or pair) produces a different colourway. The moment all the lamps in the room light up simultaneously is the kind of session people remember.
