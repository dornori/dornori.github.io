⏱️ The Build — 90 Minutes of Real Engagement

  • Minutes 0–15: Discovery — "Whoa, what's all this?" Lay everything out. Identify parts. Read the intro together. The motor looks serious. The Wago connectors look like something from a rocket ship.
  • Minutes 15–45: Assembly — Snap the base together. Mount the motor. Thread the pole through the gear housing. This is spatial reasoning happening in real time. "Dad, I can do this part myself!"
  • Minutes 45–70: Wiring — Connect the motor, lever switch, power switch, and LED using the color-coded harness and Wago connectors. No soldering. No tools. But real wire, real terminals, a real complete circuit. "So when I flip this... the motor gets power?" Yes. Exactly that.
  • Minutes 70–90: First Light 🚀 — Flip the lever. The motor hums. The pole rises. The glowing UFO (or Amanita) climbs into the air above the table. "I MADE THIS."
