Cookie Policy

Effective Date: March 31, 2026  ·  Document version 1.0

This Cookie Policy explains how dornori.com uses cookies and similar technologies. Under the EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR, we require your prior, freely given consent for all non-essential cookies. You may withdraw or change your consent at any time.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide information to the website operator. Similar technologies include web beacons, pixel tags, local storage, and session storage.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Enable essential website functionality (shopping cart, authentication)
  • Maintain session integrity and security
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Support newsletter subscription operations
  • Analyse website usage and performance
  • Detect and prevent fraudulent activity

4. Your Consent

When you first visit dornori.com, a cookie consent banner will request your consent for non-essential cookies. The banner requires a positive action — you must actively accept optional cookies. Rejecting optional cookies is as easy as accepting them.

Your consent choices are stored and respected on subsequent visits. You can withdraw or change your consent at any time by:

  • Clicking "Cookie Settings" in the website footer
  • Adjusting your browser's cookie settings (see Section 5)
  • Contacting us at privacy@dornori.com

5. Managing Cookies via Your Browser

Most browsers allow you to view, manage, delete, and block cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies will impair Website functionality:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

6. Children & Cookies

We do not use analytics, advertising, or profiling cookies in relation to users we identify as children (under 13 in the US; under 16 in the EU/UK). Parental consent is required before any non-essential cookies are placed in connection with a child's use of our services. See our Children's Privacy Notice.

7. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our Website are set by third parties. These third parties may collect data about your online activity across different websites. Third parties placing cookies on our site include Cloudflare and analytics providers. We do not control these third parties' tracking technologies.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy at any time. Material changes will be notified via the Website. The effective date above reflects the most recent revision.

9. Contact
