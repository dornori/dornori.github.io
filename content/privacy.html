Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 31, 2026  ·  Document version 1.0

This policy applies globally and covers all products and services offered through dornori.com. The legal operator of this website is identified by the ICANN-registered Administrative Registrant for the domain dornori.com. It is intended to comply with GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, COPPA, UK GDPR, and other applicable data protection laws.

1. Definitions

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

"Controller" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data — in this case, the ICANN-registered operator of dornori.com.

"User" means any individual accessing the Website or purchasing products or services.

"Child" / "Minor" means any individual under 13 years of age (US / COPPA), under 16 years of age (EU / GDPR), or such higher age as required by applicable local law.

2. Scope & Applicability

This Policy applies to all data collected through dornori.com and any associated subdomains, mobile applications, and hardware device interactions. It applies regardless of the User's country of residence, subject to additional jurisdiction-specific rights described herein.

Applicable law frameworks include, but are not limited to:

  • EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR 2016/679)
  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act (CCPA/CPRA)
  • US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
  • EU/UK ePrivacy Directive (Cookie Law)
  • Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)
  • Australia's Privacy Act 1988
  • Brazil's Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD)

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Information Provided Directly

You may provide Personal Data when you:

  • Create an account or user profile
  • Subscribe to our newsletter or marketing communications
  • Place an order for hardware products
  • Contact us via email or support channels
  • Submit product reviews or feedback
  • Participate in promotions or surveys

This may include: full name, email address, postal/shipping address, billing address, phone number (if voluntarily provided), and any content included in communications.

3.2 Automatically Collected Data

When you access the Website, we may automatically collect:

  • IP address and approximate geolocation
  • Browser type, version, and language settings
  • Device identifiers and operating system
  • Referring URLs and exit pages
  • Pages viewed, time spent, and interaction data
  • Hardware device identifiers (where applicable)

3.3 Transaction Data

When you purchase hardware products, we collect:

  • Billing and shipping information
  • Order details, quantities, and pricing
  • Payment confirmation tokens (not full card numbers)
  • Delivery and fulfilment records

Payment card data is processed exclusively by PCI-DSS compliant third-party processors (Stripe, PayPal). We do not store full payment credentials on our systems.

3.4 Hardware Device Data

Where Dornori hardware products connect to digital services or applications, we may collect device usage data, firmware version information, and interaction logs. Specific data collection for each product is detailed in the product documentation.

5. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR)

Where GDPR or UK GDPR applies, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Newsletter subscriptions, optional analytics tracking, marketing cookies.
  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): Order fulfilment, account management, customer support.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Tax records, fraud reporting, regulatory compliance.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Website security, fraud prevention, service improvement — subject to balancing tests.

Where we rely on legitimate interests, Users have the right to object. See Section 11 for details.

6. Children's Privacy

Important: Dornori sells hardware products that may be purchased as gifts for or used by children. We take children's privacy seriously. Full details are in our separate Children's Privacy Notice.

We do not knowingly collect Personal Data directly from children under 13 (US) or under 16 (EU/UK) without verified parental consent. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with Personal Data without your consent, please contact privacy@dornori.com immediately.

Where products are designed for or marketed to children, we apply enhanced privacy protections including:

  • No behavioural advertising to children
  • No sale of children's data to third parties
  • Parental access and deletion rights
  • Compliance with COPPA, GDPR-K, and the UK Children's Code

7. Newsletter & Marketing Communications

By subscribing to our newsletter, you expressly consent to receive recurring emails which may include product updates, promotional offers, and informational content. You may withdraw consent and unsubscribe at any time via the unsubscribe link included in every email or by contacting privacy@dornori.com.

We do not send marketing emails to addresses associated with accounts where the user is identified as a child.

We use third-party email service providers. All providers are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and in compliance with applicable law.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies. For full details, including a cookie inventory, consent mechanism, and opt-out instructions, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

Essential cookies (required for the Website to function) are placed without consent. All other cookies require your prior, freely given, specific, and informed consent, which you may manage or withdraw at any time.

9. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your Personal Data. We may disclose data to:

  • Infrastructure providers: Cloudflare (CDN, DDoS protection, DNS)
  • Payment processors: Stripe, PayPal — for transaction processing
  • Email and newsletter services: For delivery of subscribed communications
  • Analytics providers: Aggregated/anonymised where possible
  • Logistics and fulfilment partners: For shipping and delivery of hardware products
  • Legal authorities: When required by applicable law, court order, or to protect rights

All third-party processors are bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) and may not use your data for their own purposes. A full Subprocessor List is maintained and updated upon material changes.

10. International Data Transfers

Your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence, including countries that may not provide the same level of data protection as your home country.

Where such transfers occur from the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, we implement appropriate safeguards including:

  • EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs)
  • Adequacy decisions where applicable
