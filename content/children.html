Child Safety & COPPA / GDPR-K

Children's Privacy Notice

Effective Date: March 31, 2026  ·  Document version 1.0

For parents and guardians: Dornori takes children's privacy seriously. We sell hardware products that may be purchased as gifts for or used by children. This notice explains how we handle any data relating to children and the rights you have as a parent or guardian. Contact us immediately at privacy@dornori.com if you have concerns.

1. Scope & Who This Applies To

This Children's Privacy Notice applies to any use of dornori.com by or on behalf of a child, and to hardware products that are marketed toward or used by children. It supplements our main Privacy Policy and applies in addition to it.

For the purposes of this Notice, a "child" or "minor" means:

  • Any person under 13 years of age (United States — COPPA)
  • Any person under 16 years of age (European Union / UK — GDPR / UK GDPR)
  • Such higher age as may be required by applicable local law

2. Applicable Law

This Notice is designed to comply with:

  • COPPA — US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (15 U.S.C. § 6501 et seq.)
  • GDPR Art. 8 — Child's consent to information society services (age 16, or lower where member states permit)
  • UK GDPR Art. 8 and the UK Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code)
  • EU DSA — Digital Services Act provisions on minors

3. Our Approach to Children's Data

Dornori does not direct the dornori.com website at children and does not knowingly solicit or collect Personal Data directly from children. Our website is intended for adults (including parents and guardians) who may purchase products for use by children.

Where hardware products are used by children, the parent or guardian who created the account and made the purchase is the data subject for all account-level data. Data associated with a child's use of a product is described in the relevant product documentation.

4. Data We Do Not Collect from Children

We do not knowingly:

  • Create accounts in a child's name
  • Collect a child's email address, name, or contact information
  • Use children's data for behavioural advertising or profiling
  • Sell children's Personal Data to third parties
  • Use persistent identifiers to track children across websites or services

5. Parental Consent

Where any service or product feature would involve direct collection of Personal Data from a child, we will first seek verifiable parental consent in accordance with applicable law before any data collection occurs. Methods of verifiable parental consent may include:

  • Email confirmation with verifiable parental identity checks
  • Credit card verification for parental identity
  • Signed parental consent forms (where required)

We will not condition a child's participation in an activity on the disclosure of more Personal Data than is reasonably necessary.

6. Parental Rights

As a parent or guardian, you have the right to:

  • Review any Personal Data we have collected in connection with your child
  • Request deletion of your child's Personal Data
  • Refuse further collection of your child's Personal Data
  • Withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@dornori.com with the subject line "Parental Request — [Child Data]". We will respond within 30 days and may require verification of your identity and relationship to the child.

7. No Marketing to Children

We do not send marketing communications to email addresses we know or have reason to believe belong to a child. If a parent subscribes to our newsletter, they may receive product information that may be relevant to family purchases, but we do not address or target marketing at children.

We do not serve behavioural advertising to users we identify as children. Where analytics cookies are used, they are not applied to users we have reason to believe are children.

8. Default Settings for Children's Safety

In accordance with the UK Children's Code and GDPR best practices, where our services may be accessed by children:

  • Privacy settings default to the most protective option
  • No profiling, tracking, or non-essential data collection is activated by default
  • Geolocation data is not collected from children's device usage
  • Children's usage data is not shared with third parties beyond what is strictly necessary for product operation

9. Product Safety Information

All Dornori hardware products comply with applicable product safety standards, including EU EN 71 (toy safety), CE marking requirements, and applicable US CPSC regulations where products are intended for or accessible to children under 14 years of age. Safety information is included with all products and available at support@dornori.com.

10. If You Believe We Have Inadvertently Collected a Child's Data

If you are a parent or guardian and believe that your child has submitted Personal Data to us without your consent, please contact us immediately at privacy@dornori.com. We will delete such information from our records as quickly as practicable and no later than within 30 days of notification.

11. Changes to This Notice

We will notify parents and guardians of material changes to this Notice via email (where we hold an email address associated with a child's account or product registration) and by posting the updated Notice on this page.

12. Contact

  • Children's Privacy Contact: privacy@dornori.com
  • Subject line: "Children's Privacy — [Your inquiry]"
  • Response time: Within 30 days

For US COPPA concerns, you may also contact the Federal Trade Commission at ftc.gov.
