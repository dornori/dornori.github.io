Children's Privacy Notice

Effective Date: March 31, 2026

Dornori sells hardware products that may be purchased as gifts for or used by children. This notice explains how we handle data relating to children and the rights you have as a parent or guardian. Contact us immediately at privacy@dornori.com if you have concerns. This notice supplements our main Privacy Policy.

1. Scope & Definitions

A "child" or "minor" means: any person under 13 years of age (United States — COPPA); any person under 16 years of age (EU / UK — GDPR / UK GDPR); or such higher age as required by applicable local law. This notice applies to any use of dornori.com by or on behalf of a child, and to hardware products marketed toward or used by children.

2. Applicable Law

This notice is designed to comply with: COPPA (US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, 15 U.S.C. § 6501 et seq.); GDPR Art. 8 (EU); UK GDPR Art. 8 and the UK Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code); and EU DSA provisions on minors.

3. Our Approach

Dornori does not direct the Website at children and does not knowingly solicit or collect Personal Data directly from children. The Website is intended for adults, including parents and guardians who may purchase products for use by children. Where hardware products are used by children, the parent or guardian who created the account is the data subject for all account-level data.

4. Data We Do Not Collect from Children

We do not knowingly: create accounts in a child's name; collect a child's email address, name, or contact information; use children's data for behavioural advertising or profiling; sell children's Personal Data to third parties; or use persistent identifiers to track children across websites or services.

5. Parental Consent

Where any feature would involve direct collection of Personal Data from a child, we will first seek verifiable parental consent before any data collection occurs. Methods may include email confirmation with identity verification, or credit card verification for parental identity. We will not condition a child's participation on the disclosure of more Personal Data than is reasonably necessary.

6. Parental Rights

As a parent or guardian you have the right to: review any Personal Data we hold in connection with your child; request deletion of your child's Personal Data; refuse further collection; and withdraw consent at any time. To exercise these rights, contact privacy@dornori.com with the subject line "Parental Request". We will respond within 30 days and may require verification of your identity and relationship to the child.

7. No Marketing to Children

We do not send marketing communications to addresses we know or have reason to believe belong to a child. We do not serve behavioural advertising to users we identify as children. We do not apply analytics cookies to users we have reason to believe are children.

8. Default Safety Settings

Where our services may be accessed by children: privacy settings default to the most protective option; no profiling, tracking, or non-essential data collection is activated by default; geolocation data is not collected from children's device usage; and children's usage data is not shared with third parties beyond what is strictly necessary for product operation.

9. Product Safety

Dornori hardware products comply with applicable product safety standards including EU EN 71 (toy safety), CE marking requirements, and applicable US CPSC regulations where products are intended for or accessible to children under 14. Safety information is included with all products and available at support@dornori.com.

10. Inadvertent Collection

If you believe your child has submitted Personal Data to us without your consent, please contact privacy@dornori.com immediately. We will delete such information from our records within 30 days of notification.

11. Changes

We will notify parents and guardians of material changes to this notice via email where we hold a relevant address, and by posting the updated notice on this page.

12. Contact

Children's privacy inquiries: privacy@dornori.com (subject: "Children's Privacy")
US COPPA concerns may also be directed to the FTC at ftc.gov.
